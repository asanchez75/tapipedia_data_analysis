  1. Agriculture, forestry, and fishing, value added (% of GDP):

  2. Employment in agriculture:

    • The level of employment in the agricultural sector directly impacts the livelihoods of rural populations. An AIS can use this indicator to understand the labor force involved in agriculture and tailor information and services to meet their needs, such as access to training, market information, and agricultural best practices.

  3. Ease of starting a business:

    • This indicator measures the regulatory and bureaucratic hurdles faced by individuals trying to start agricultural businesses. An AIS can play a role in providing information on regulatory requirements, business planning, and access to financial resources to support new agricultural ventures.

  4. Gender gap in mobile ownership:

    • Understanding the gender gap in mobile ownership is essential for ensuring equitable access to agricultural information and services. An AIS can use this indicator to design outreach programs that specifically target and address the needs of female farmers, potentially through mobile-based information delivery systems.

  5. ICT skills (Individuals with standard skills):

    • This indicator reflects the level of information and communication technology (ICT) skills within the population. An AIS can tailor its information dissemination and communication strategies based on the ICT skills of the target audience, ensuring effective and meaningful engagement.

  6. Individuals using the Internet:

    • The level of Internet usage among the population is crucial for determining the viability of digital platforms for delivering agricultural information and services. An AIS can leverage this indicator to shape its digital outreach and ensure that information is accessible to those who utilize online resources.

  7. R&D Agriculture Government investments, forestry, fishing (Central Government):

    • Government investments in research and development (R&D) for agriculture, forestry, and fishing can directly influence the availability of innovative agricultural technologies and practices. An AIS can benefit from understanding R&D investments to promote and disseminate the adoption of new agricultural techniques and technologies.

  8. Mobile cellular subscriptions (per 100 people):

    • Access to mobile communication platforms is vital for reaching rural populations with agricultural information. An AIS can use this indicator to assess the potential reach of mobile-based agricultural advisory services and optimize communication strategies.

  9. Percentage of graduates from tertiary education graduating from Agriculture:

    • The percentage of graduates specializing in agriculture can influence the availability of skilled labor and expertise in the agricultural sector. An AIS can collaborate with educational institutions to bridge the gap between academic knowledge and practical agricultural skills, potentially through extension services and training programs.

  10. Research and development expenditure (% of GDP):

    • Government expenditure on research and development directly impacts the innovation and advancement of agricultural practices. An AIS can leverage R&D expenditure data to inform farmers about new technologies and best practices that result from such investments.

  11. Researchers in R&D (per million people):

    • The presence of researchers involved in agricultural R&D can lead to the development and dissemination of cutting-edge agricultural knowledge and technologies. An AIS can use this information to engage with and disseminate the latest research findings to farming communities.

  12. Rural population:

    • The size and characteristics of the rural population are crucial for understanding the target audience of an AIS. By knowing the demographic and geographical distribution of the rural population, an AIS can tailor its services and information to address specific regional needs and challenges.

In conclusion, these indicators can impact an Agricultural Information System by providing valuable insights that can guide the design, implementation, and customization of agricultural information and services to effectively meet the needs of farmers, rural communities, and agricultural stakeholders.
